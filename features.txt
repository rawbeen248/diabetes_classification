The explanation of each feature and their normal/dangerous levels:

Glucose:
    It's the sugar in your blood that gives your body energy.
    Normal: 70-100 mg/dL.
    Dangerous: Above 126 mg/dL (may indicate diabetes).

Blood Pressure:
    Measures the force of blood in your arteries.
    Normal: Around 120/80 mm Hg.
    Dangerous: Consistently 130/80 mm Hg or higher (hypertension).

Skin Thickness:
    Measures skinfold thickness (used in body fat assessment).
    Normal: Varies, but roughly 10-20 mm.
    No specific dangerous levels.

Insulin:
    Regulates blood sugar levels.
    Normal: 5-15 Î¼IU/mL.
    Dangerous: Very low (type 1 diabetes) or very high (type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance).

BMI (Body Mass Index):
    Measures body fat based on height and weight.
    Normal: 18.5-24.9.
    Dangerous: Below 18.5 (underweight) or above 30 (obese).

Diabetes Pedigree Function:
    Estimates diabetes risk based on family history.
